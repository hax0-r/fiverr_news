Chup Online: The World’s First Breakthrough Ending Patient Frustration in Healthcare

Lauren Forristal   /   2:00 PM PDT • September 10, 2024

  Comment

Image Credits: Chup Online

In Malaysia, waiting for a doctor’s appointment is a common source of frustration. Clinics and hospitals are often bogged down by unpredictable schedules—doctors running late, unscheduled emergencies, and patients showing up early or not at all. For years, queue management systems have been introduced to address this issue, but they’ve all fallen short. The reason? They rely on a level of punctuality that rarely exists in real-world healthcare environments.

That’s where Chup Online, a Malaysian-based Dynamic Queue Management and Appointment Automation system, has stepped in. Instead of trying to force patients and doctors into rigid time slots, Chup Online adjusts to reality—it adapts to delays, emergencies, and unpredictability, all while keeping patients informed. It’s not about fixing the queue but about fixing the communication gap.

The Simple Solution: Just Keep Patients Informed

Patients don’t necessarily mind delays; they mind not knowing. This is the core problem Chup Online solves. With their system, patients are notified one hour before their scheduled appointment if their doctor is running late. Instead of arriving at a hospital and wasting hours in the waiting room, patients can adjust their arrival time based on real-time updates delivered via WhatsApp or SMS. “Patients don’t need miracles; they just need to know when to show up,” says Nik Abbas, Chief Product Officer at Chup Online. The system allows healthcare providers to keep patients informed without adding extra work for staff. Instead of having nurses or administrative staff manually call hundreds of patients to rearrange their appointments, Chup Online automates the entire process.

Why Other Systems Don’t Work?

The failure of traditional systems in Malaysia boils down to one thing: they assume everyone is on time. But doctors frequently need to take unscheduled breaks for surgeries or consultations, throwing the entire day’s schedule off course. Meanwhile, patients are left in the dark, unsure of when—or if—they’ll be seen. Chup Online’s Dynamic Queue Management and Appointment Automation changes the game. When a doctor is delayed for any reason, the system automatically reschedules the rest of the day’s appointments and informs patients accordingly. It’s an elegant solution to a messy problem.

Behind the Scenes: A Complex Solution to a Simple Problem

While Dynamic Queue Management sounds straightforward in theory, executing it at the scale of a busy hospital is anything but simple. The system not only has to account for clinic closing times, patient consent, and clashes with walk-ins, but also manage the complexity of no-shows and patients who are already on-site. But that’s just scratching the surface. Chup Online had to tackle an even more intricate challenge: the bottlenecks at every station patients pass through before even seeing a doctor. This includes registration, triage, blood tests, billing, pharmacy, and even the insurance counter. The system ensures patients arrive just in time to meet their doctor and can seamlessly transition from one station to the next.

To achieve this, Chup Online didn’t just focus on queue management—it had to automate the entire patient process at every station. This required developing features that hospitals’ Hospital Information Systems (HIS) should have had, ensuring that every interaction—whether it’s processing a test or completing a payment—was optimized for speed and efficiency. The goal? To make sure the patient’s journey is as fast and smooth as possible, from start to finish. This level of integration took years of real-world testing, multiple iterations, and fine-tuning algorithms to accommodate every delay, emergency, and bottleneck in a hospital’s daily operations.

Results: 2-Hour Wait Times Reduced to 15 Minutes

At UKM Specialist Centre, a private hospital where Chup Online was piloted, the results were significant. Average patient wait times dropped from almost two hours to just 15 minutes. Over 92.34% of patients reported being highly satisfied with the system, particularly appreciating the real-time updates that allowed them to arrive exactly when needed. Now, Chup Online is conducting a pilot project with Malaysia’s Ministry of Health, and the results have been equally promising. While not every patient can be “rescued”—especially in last-minute emergencies—the vast majority now spend far less time in waiting rooms. With the company’s plan to roll out nationwide to over 3,000 clinics and 150 hospitals, Chup Online is poised to become one of the nation’s most-used apps after the pilot project.

Why No One Else Tackles This Problem?

Most companies don’t want to touch this problem because it takes too much time and investment to solve properly. Building a Dynamic Queue Management and Appointment Automation system that functions in real time is complex, requiring massive investment. Other systems focus on simpler, easier-to-implement features. But Chup Online recognized this was a pain point worth addressing. “Other systems don’t want to invest years in fixing this one problem. We did, and it’s starting to pay off,” says Nik Abbas.

The Future: Expanding Beyond Healthcare

Having proven its effectiveness in Malaysia, Chup Online is now eyeing expansion. The company plans to roll out its Dynamic Queue Management and Appointment Automation system in Indonesia, Thailand, and the Philippines, where similar healthcare punctuality issues persist. But the solution isn’t limited to healthcare alone. Chup Online can also be applied to other public services such as immigration, road tax, and other government departments, as well as private services like in-person banking, restaurants, salons, spas, and workshops—anywhere waiting times and unpredictability create friction. As Southeast Asia’s healthcare systems and public services continue to expand, Chup Online’s real-time, patient- and customer-centric approach could be exactly what the region needs.

The Mission: A World with No On-Premise Waits

Chup Online’s mission is simple: a world with no on-premise waits anymore. The algorithm is designed to handle any situation. Whether booking a healthcare appointment, visiting a restaurant, or going to the bank, Chup Online makes sure you don’t have to wait. In Malay slang, “Chup” means “to book it”, and with Chup Online, once you “Chup it,” there’s no need to wait.
